Why Did I Make A Website?

I promise you, I'm not trying to break in as a front, middle, and back end developer. So, this isn't my portfolio and is quite basic. I honestly should be doing statistical projects instead but web development was more alluring. Still have so much to go, especially making a webpage "responsive" (like using additional CSS units besides "px) and some other cool stuff, like React.js ?? Not to mention the theory behind all this, which is important because I have a computer science minor and really understanding it can help me solve why I don't like eating cherry tomatoes. Overall, pretty nice to learn HTML, CSS, and Javascript. My summer project, I suppose. A part of me likes to build things from the ground up, and I was always curious how websites and built and decided to go for it. Lots of videos to learn from. But now to the big reason. I could go on and on on some bulls**t that I'm a curious boi but that's a lie. Now, here's the major reason. A project in my CS class during my senior year of college needed us to add on an 'user interface' or something that required a webpage to display our project. It was a search engine and we had to display results somehow. Well, I was the only one who didn't know HTML and was so confused and helpless. Lot of times during my CS group projects I was the village idiot but made sure to contribute something, even if it was basic stuff. Very embarassing and so it was on the back of mind that one day I'll learn HTML. Now, had no idea JavaScript and CSS were involved and probably much more. My page is not that responsive, so please view in full browser for best experience please and thanks. It's also important to drink water, please drink some water. I don't want this page to become my political thoughts but flavored sparkling water is a joke, just drink regular sparkling water. I like regular sparkling water :D.
